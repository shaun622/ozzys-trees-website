Tree services in Chermside

Chermside is our home base, so a job here usually means we are a few streets away rather than driving in from the other side of Brisbane. We work right across the suburb, from the post-war homes on the old War Service blocks near Sparkes Paddock to the newer townhouses and unit complexes that have gone up around Westfield and Gympie Road.

The older parts of Chermside have had decades for trees to get big. A lot of the back yards on the 1960s blocks now carry mature gums, leopard trees and the odd poinciana that has outgrown the space it was planted in. We handle the lot here: tree removal, tree lopping and pruning, stump grinding, palm work, and storm clean-ups when the Northside cops a summer thunderstorm.

What we do here

Every tree job in Chermside, sorted

Most Chermside yards we get called to have the usual Northside mix. Tall spotted gums and other eucalypts that drop limbs and litter gutters, the occasional large fig or leopard tree near the front fence, and plenty of palms, mostly cocos and queen palms, that have got too tall to clean from a ladder. Out towards Chermside Hills and the Raven Street Reserve end of the suburb the bushland is open eucalypt forest, so homes backing onto it often want gums thinned, deadwood taken out, or trees cleared back off the roof and the fence line.

Block sizes vary a fair bit, which changes how we approach a job. On the tighter newer subdivisions and the unit sites we will often bring the cherry picker so we can take a tree down in sections without dropping anything on a shed, a pool or the neighbour's fence. The mini track loader and the tracked stump grinder fit through a standard side gate, so we can grind stumps out the back without tearing up the lawn getting there.
